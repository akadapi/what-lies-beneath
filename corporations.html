The Conflict of Interest

The ISA is both the regulator and the promoter.

The International Seabed Authority's mandate under UNCLOS requires it to both regulate deep sea mining and promote the development of ocean mineral resources. Critics argue this creates a fundamental conflict of interest.

Germany holds an ISA exploration contract in the CCZ while simultaneously calling for a moratorium. France does too. The ISA is partly funded by the contractors it regulates — and has repeatedly delayed binding environmental rules while continuing to issue exploration contracts.

Sources: ISA (isa.org.jm) · Congress.gov CRS Report IG10053, Feb 2026 · Wikipedia — International Seabed Authority
